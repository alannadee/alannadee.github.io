### welcome, I'm so glad you're here!

And when I say I'm so glad you're here, I mean it. like, I'm *really* glad you're here. 

You reading this right now, at this moment in time, means that I have somehow succeeded in developing my very own personal website, and I have (somehow) successfully deployed it for the world to see. 

I started this blog post on 23/06/2025, and I intend to use it as a dump of all my thoughts during the creation process, and as a place I can refer back to while developing so that I can remember exactly why I am doing this, and why I have gaslit myself into believing it is worth all the pain and suffering. 

## what exactly is this site?

To be perfectly frank, and very honest, I would say I have subzero experience with proper development. 

Sure, I've dabbled in some HTML/CSS here and there, passed some papers, did my best to contribute (feebly) to a hackathon project or two - but when it comes to real personal projects, or anything that's actually usable in real life, my experience is nil. 

Also, I thought it would be *really* **really** cool to have a little corner of the internet to store all my random ramblings (my friends are sick of my yapping), and to showcase my various hobbies (photography, anyone?).

This website is a showcase of all my time and suffering. An intersem break project that hopefully gets finished (PLEASE let the publishing date of this article be pre August 2025). So please keep that in mind while perusing. Blood, sweat and tears were shed to present to you whatever this is. 

## why didn't you just use HTML and CSS?

Good question. 

Making a whole website in HTML and CSS would most definitely have taken me a **lot** less time, and I probably also would have enjoyed it more. But that would have been staying in my comfort zone, which, in my mind, completely absolutely defeats the purpose of doing a personal project.

So I've decided to throw myself in the deep end, and make a website on React and TypeScript, with the help of Tailwind (which is getting a lot of getting used to). 

The negative experience I have in the likes of React and JavaScript result in whatever a mess this site is. My friends probably have a lot to say about what I've done, and how many bad decisions I've made during the process - but the important thing is *something* made it out of VSCode, and hopefully I'll be able to go back and improve on the little things later (structure, hardcoding, efficiency... there's a LOT of places I don't know what I'm doing and I BEG you not to look at my GitHub)
